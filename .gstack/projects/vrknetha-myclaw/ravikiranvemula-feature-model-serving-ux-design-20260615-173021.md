## Problem Statement

Nadella's thesis: in an AI economy a firm's moat is a **learning loop** that compounds **human capital** (people's judgment, tacit knowledge) and **token capital** (the firm's own AI capability) — a loop the firm *owns*, so it can swap a generalist model without losing its "company veteran." Gantry today is a strong model-serving + agent runtime (provider-neutral DeepAgents harness, memory, agent-authored skills, durable runs/events, per-app isolation), but it does **not yet compound org-specific learning into owned IP**. It serves models; it does not yet make every org's institutional knowledge queryable, measurable, and improvable on a loop. The objective: turn Gantry into the platform that gives every org its own learning loop on top of swappable models.

## Demand Evidence

This is a strategic/platform bet, not a customer-validated feature — be honest about that. The supporting evidence is (1) strong industry consensus that the learning loop is the defensible moat ([The Learning Loop is the Moat](https://www.agentico.ai/post/the-learning-loop-is-the-moat)), (2) the data-flywheel pattern is already blueprinted and shipping in production (NVIDIA: distill production traces into 1B–8B models at ~98% lower cost / 70× lower latency, [blueprint](https://github.com/NVIDIA-AI-Blueprints/data-flywheel); [Shopify](https://shopify.engineering/fine-tuning-agent-shopify-flow); [Agent-in-the-Loop](https://arxiv.org/html/2510.06674v2)), and (3) the operator's own conviction. **Open gap:** no Gantry customer has yet asked for this; the first real validation step is in The Assignment.

## Status Quo

What Gantry has today (the loop substrate, ~70% present):
- Provider-neutral DeepAgents harness — **already passes Nadella's "swap the generalist" test** (engine derived from provider; models are swappable).
- Memory (extractor / dreaming / consolidation), agent-authored skills, durable run/event store, tool-call + correction traces (permission denials, edits), per-app/org isolation.

What's missing for the loop:
- **Structured trace-as-dataset:** runs/events/memory exist but are not a first-class, queryable, exportable, labeled interaction dataset.
- **Private evals:** no org-owned eval suite grounded in real outcomes, runnable against any model, tracking improvement over time.
- **Active elicitation:** agents have static personas + passive memory; they do not deliberately ask the criteria/edge-case questions that externalize tacit knowledge, nor consolidate answers into reusable assets.
- **Distill/RL env + owned ELM:** no path from curated traces to an org-owned small model (later phase).

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

User: a team/org running Gantry whose expertise lives in people's heads and in messy conversations. Narrowest wedge (this design = Approach A): **make the loop observable and measurable first** — structured trace capture + a private eval suite + elicitation-v1. No model training yet. This is the smallest thing that turns "we serve models" into "we own a measurable learning loop," and it is the prerequisite for everything downstream.

## Constraints

- Reuse Gantry's existing substrate (run/event store, memory consolidation, skill-authoring, harness, model catalog) — avoid net-new infra.
- Strict per-org IP isolation: traces, evals, and any future model are the org's, never cross-tenant, never used to train a shared model (this is the whole point of "owning the loop").
- Provider-neutral: evals must run against any configured model/lane (Anthropic SDK or DeepAgents).
- No premature training: distill/RL is gated on trace volume + an eval/outcome signal.
- Security/privacy: capture must respect existing redaction (on capture AND export); corrections are high-signal but may contain sensitive data.
- Eval scoring calls external providers — verify provider zero-retention / no-train settings for the eval egress path. Per-org isolation is not only a Gantry-internal property here.

## Premises (confirmed with user)

1. **The IP is the LOOP** (capture → eval → improve → deploy) the org owns — not the weights or even the raw data. Gantry = the loop platform on swappable models.
2. **Day-1 actions = structured trace capture + private evals.** Distill/SFT/RL are later phases, gated on volume + an eval signal. Do NOT fine-tune day 1 (no baseline, no quality filter, trains on noise, can't prove improvement).
3. **Don't remove personas — evolve them.** A generic "how-to-elicit" skill (knowledge-engineer behavior) + a learned, growing domain profile (memory + consolidated skills + scenario/criteria library). Memory is necessary but not sufficient: recall captures facts, not judgment/criteria/edge-cases (the tacit part).

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Loop Foundation — Capture + Private Evals + Elicitation v1 (CHOSEN)
Structured, queryable, exportable interaction dataset (auto-labeled category+scenario, human-confirmed on corrections) + private eval suite runnable against any model + a generic elicitation skill that makes agents ask criteria/edge-case questions and consolidate answers into memory + draft skills. No training. Effort M, Risk Low. Reuses run/event store, memory, skills, harness, catalog.

### Approach B: Full Compounding Loop — A + Distill/RL + Owned ELM (ROADMAP)
A, then close the loop: a private distill/RL env turning eval-gated high-quality traces (corrections + evals as reward) into an org-owned small model, re-distillable onto any base. Effort XL, Risk Med-High. The destination, premature before A exists.

### Approach C: Elicitation-First Wedge (FOLDED INTO A)
Lead with a knowledge-elicitation agent that interviews experts in-flow and structures externalization into a KB + skills + evals. Effort M-L, Risk Med. Its elicitation behavior becomes A's "elicitation-v1"; can be sharpened into a standalone wedge later.

## Recommended Approach

**Approach A**, because it is exactly what the confirmed premises demand (capture + evals first), it is the lowest-risk path (mostly wiring Gantry's existing substrate), it makes the loop measurable (the eval suite is the "is it improving on OUR outcomes" signal Nadella stresses), and it unblocks B. B is documented as the roadmap destination; C's elicitation is folded in as elicitation-v1.

**Pre-slice-1 blocking decision** (resolve via `/plan-eng-review` BEFORE slice 1, not an open question): is the dataset a **projection table** (decoupled schema, more work) or a **view over the event store** (cheaper, couples to the event schema)? This determines whether capture is a read-model or a write-path change.

Build slices (each independently shippable):
1. **Trace capture → dataset:** normalize existing runs/events/tool-calls/corrections into a per-org, queryable dataset, exported as plain **JSONL of normalized traces** (a training-ready export schema is deferred to B — don't design for a consumer that doesn't exist yet). Label each trace with `category` (the kind of work — e.g. `code-review`, `support-reply`) + `scenario` (the situation — e.g. `user rejected the agent's suggested fix`) via a cheap LLM labeler. **Signal tiers:** explicit corrections (user edits/rejections) = human-confirmed, highest signal; implicit signals (retries, permission denials) = weak/heuristic, NOT treated as ground-truth labels; auto-labels elsewhere = low-confidence (spot-check accuracy). **Redaction MUST apply on capture AND export** (slice-1 acceptance item).
2. **Private evals:** promote real traces into eval cases — **deterministic checks where possible; LLM-as-judge with a fixed rubric** for open-ended cases. Seed corpus = The Assignment's 10 hand-built cases. Run the suite against any configured model; track pass-rate over time. **Judge determinism is required:** pin the judge model + temperature, prefer deterministic checks, and report confidence on small suites so judge variance can't masquerade as model improvement. v1 surfaces results to the **operator only** (agent-facing eval feedback is out of scope for v1).
3. **Elicitation-v1:** a generic elicitation skill + prompt nudge so agents recognize a knowledge gap, ask criteria/edge-case questions, and consolidate answers into memory + a **draft skill** (one term throughout). Drafts go through `request_skill_proposal` review — **never auto-installed** (per the reviewed-runtime-tools contract). Negative path: if the user ignores/declines a question, don't re-ask; tune against a "questions accepted" rate. Keep personas; layer the evolving domain profile on top (defer the domain-profile layer if the generic skill alone proves the slice).

## Open Questions

- Where does the dataset physically live and how is it exported (new table vs. a view over the event store; export format for downstream training)?
- Eval authoring UX: operator-curated, agent-proposed, or auto-mined from corrections — and who approves?
- Elicitation cadence: how aggressive should agents be about asking questions before it annoys users? (Tune against a "questions accepted" rate, per the research.)
- Reward signal for B: are corrections + eval pass/fail a strong enough reward, or is explicit preference labeling needed?
- IP/privacy: redaction policy for traces that become training data; per-org opt-in.

## Success Criteria

- Slice 1: one team's real conversations captured as a labeled dataset (category+scenario), queryable + exportable; corrections flagged as high-signal.
- Slice 2: a private eval suite of ≥10 real-trace-derived cases runs against ≥2 different models and reports a pass-rate that moves when the model/prompt changes — with a pinned judge, so the movement reflects the model under test, not judge variance.
- Slice 3: agents demonstrably ask elicitation questions on a knowledge gap and produce ≥1 consolidated skill/scenario from the answers.
- Loop proof (later, B): a distilled small model matches or beats the generalist on the org's private evals — the "swap the generalist, keep the veteran" test, made literal.

## The Assignment

Before building any pipeline: take **one week of real conversation traces from one team** using Gantry, and by hand turn the **corrections** in them (where someone fixed or rejected the agent) into **10 private eval cases** (input + what the right outcome was). Then run those 10 cases against two different models through the harness. This is a one-day manual exercise that validates the single most important assumption — *that your corrections carry enough signal to measure "is the model getting better at OUR work"* — before you invest in the capture+eval pipeline. If the 10 hand-built evals already discriminate between models, the loop thesis holds and slice 1 is justified. If they don't, you've learned the signal isn't there yet, cheaply. These 10 cases become the **seed corpus for slice 2**, and passing this gate is the explicit **go/no-go for slice 1**. Prerequisite: confirm one team's traces are currently retrievable and redaction-clean enough to hand-mine.

## What I noticed about how you think

- You didn't ask "which model is best" — you said "the real opportunity is the **learning loop on top of models**." That's the same reframe Nadella makes, and it's the one that separates a platform bet from a feature.
- You caught the hard part yourself: "the knowledge is in people's brains, unless you ask the right question even they don't speak it out." That's the externalization problem knowledge engineers spend careers on — you named it in one sentence.
- You asked "is memory enough?" instead of assuming it was. Most people ship memory and call it learning. Questioning your own mechanism is the tell.
- You wanted to "make it happen from day 1" — strong bias to action, which is right. The one correction was *sequencing*: capture + evals on day 1, training later. You kept the urgency, we just pointed it at the part that compounds.
