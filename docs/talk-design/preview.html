Design system · Talk deck · v0.1

MyClaw
built because I had to.

A literary-minimal design system for a thirty-minute conference talk. Serious, personal, legible from the back row. Typography does the work, amber carries the emphasis, everything else gets out of the way.

01 · Palette

Four colors, three of them working.

Near-black ground, warm off-white for body, amber for the emphasis, graphite for the quieter work. No gradients. No secondary. Scarcity is the point.

Ink
#0B0D0E
Slide background. Reads black under a bright projector, not harsh under a dim one.
Paper
#F5F1EA
Body text. Warm off-white, easier on the eye than pure white for long stretches.
Ember
#E8A63C
Used sparingly. Dollar amounts, thesis lines, the arrow chain, one word of italic display.
Graphite
#5C5F63
Tag-lines, footers, slide metadata. The quiet layer that gives hierarchy room to breathe.

04 · Application rules

Where ember goes. Where it doesn’t.

The system works only if the accent stays scarce. One ember moment per slide, two max. Everything else is paper on ink, or graphite for quiet metadata.

Display type

Fraunces 48–112px, weight 400. Italic only for the one emphasized noun in each headline. Never italic a whole line. Never bold.

Ember as emphasis

Reserved for: the dollar amount on slide 4, the italic word on slide 6/10, the arrow and start/end nodes of the flow motif, the memory technique names on slide 8.

Readable at 20 feet

Body text never smaller than 20px on the slide. Monospace code never smaller than 18px. Tag-lines at 11–13px are OK because nobody has to read them from row 30.

Side padding

96px minimum on 1280×720. The slide should feel like a page in a magazine, not a terminal window.

Motion

Cut or crossfade only. No builds. No typewriter effects. No kinetic typography. Trust the words.

Monospace chain

The agent ──▶ canUseTool ──▶ phone ──▶ approve motif is the single repeating visual device. Appears once on slide 7, nowhere else. Must be readable at 20 feet.
